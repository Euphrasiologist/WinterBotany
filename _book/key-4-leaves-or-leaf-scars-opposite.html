Winter Botany in the British Isles

Key 4: Leaves or leaf scars opposite

  1. Plants scrambling, climbing or parasitic on trees -> Key 4.1
  2. Plants not as above -> 3
    1. Plants evergreen -> Key 4.2
    2. Plants deciduous -> Key 4.3

Key 4.1: Climbing, or epiphytic plants parasitic on trees

  1. Plant parasitic on tree branches with twigs repeatedly forked at the nodes -> 3
  2. Plant rooted in soil -> 5
    1. Twigs sickly green … Viscum (album)
    2. Twigs brown … Loranthus (rare hortal)
  3. Evergreen (or semi-evergreen) -> 7
  4. Deciduous -> 9
    1. Leaves simple … Lonicera
    2. Leaves compound, climbing using coiling petioles … Clematis
  5. Climbing by coiling petioles, persisting in winter … Clematis
  6. Climbing by aerial roots or scrambling -> 11
    1. Anvil shaped hairs on stems, stems twining clockwise, fruits persistent with many overlapping papery bracts … Humulus (lupulus)
    2. Not as above -> 13
  7. Bundle traces 1, scrambling, weakly climbing … Jasminum (nudiflorum)
  8. Bundle traces >1 -> 15
    1. Bundle traces 3 … Lonicera
    2. Bundle traces 5 … Schizophragma (hydrangoides)

Key 4.2: Evergreen plants

  1. Leaves simple and entire -> 3
  2. Leaves compound or toothed -> 5
    1. Trailing, thin, wiry shrubs <20cm tall at maturity -> Key 4.2.1
    2. Tree or upright shrub -> Key 4.2.2
  3. Leaves ternate… Choisya (ternata)
  4. Leaves toothed -> Key 4.2.3

Key 4.2.1: Wiring, creeping, trailing shrubs

  1. Leaves with stellate hairs … Helianthemum
  2. Leaves with simple hairs or hairless -> 3
    1. Leaves translucent dotted with sunken glands, aromatic … Thymus
    2. Leaves not translucent dotted or aromatic -> 5
  3. Leaves with revolute margins -> 7
  4. Leaves with flat margins -> 11
    1. Leaves < 2mm long, petiole absent … Calluna
    2. Leaves >2mm long, petiole present -> 9
  5. Leaves linear, rounded in cross section, 2-5mm long … Frankenia
  6. Leaves oblong, 5-8mm long -> Loiseleuria
    1. Stipules absent -> 13
    2. Stipules present … Herniaria
  7. Veronica bifurcate Linnaea here

Key 4.2.2: Shrubs, trees. Leaves simple

  1. Leaves with clear 2 pinnate translucent veins on leaves -> 3
  2. Leaves with 2 pinnate veins indistinct or absent -> 19
    1. Leaves sessile -> 3
    2. Leaves petiolate -> 9
  3. Leaves <1.5cm wide and with revolute margins at maturity -> 5
  4. Leaves >2cm wide, with either transulcent dotted leaves or with minute glands attached to veinlets. Leaf scars triangular, bundle trace 1, pith spongey and excavated (hollow) -> Hypericum
  5. Leaves <4mm wide, rosemary scented. Bundle traces 3 … Rosmarinus
  6. Leaves >4mm wide (on average), odourless. Bundle traces in a transverse line … Kalmia
    1. Leaves >2cm long -> 11
    2. Leaves <2cm long … Lonicera
  7. Leaves with dendritic hairs … Phlomis
  8. Leaves with hairs simple or absent -> 13
    1. Leaves with dense silver silky hairs below … Olearia (GARRYA? LEAVES GREY WOOLLY BELOW AND UNDULATE LEAF MARGINS?)
    2. Leaves hairless both sides, except for vein axils below -> 15
  9. Vein axils below with tufts of hairs … Viburnum
  10. Leaves glabrous -> 17
    1. Sipules present, fused between petiole bases, young twigs ± square … Coprosma
    2. Stipules absent, young twigs rounded, at most angled slightly below the nodes … Ligustrum
  11. Leaves white hairy at least below, strongly aromatic -> 21
  12. Leaves never white hairy, aromatic or not -> 23
    1. Leaves hairy with long stellate hairs both sides, lavendar scented … Lavandula
    2. Leaves glabrous above, rosemary scented … Rosmarinus
  13. Twigs square, leaves odorous -> 25
  14. Twigs round, leaves odourless -> 29
    1. Leaves gland pitted one or either side -> 25
    2. Leaves not gland pitted -> Buxus (sempervirens)
  15. Young twigs green, minutely ciliate, menthol scented … Hyssopus
  16. Young twigs whitish, leaves long ciliate at base, sage scented … Satureja
    1. Leaves strongly revolute, <2mm long, sessile … Calluna
    2. Leaves not revolute -> 31
  17. Leaves fleshy, mealy grey <6cm, not valvate when developing … Atriplex
  18. Leaves not fleshy, valvate when developing … Veronica (sect Hebe)

Key 4.2.3: Shrubs, trees. Leaves toothed

  1. Twigs with dendritic hairs … Phlomis
  2. Twigs never with dendritic hairs -> 3
    1. Twigs with stellate hairs -> 5
    2. Twigs without stellate hairs -> 7
  3. Stipules present, leaves white felted below … Buddleja
  4. Stipules absent, leaves stellate hairy below … Viburnum
    1. Leaves < 2cm long, stems creeping and prostrate … Linnaea
    2. Leaves >2cm long, stems never creeping -> 9
  5. Petiole with 1 vascular bundle -> 11
  6. Petiole with 3 vascular bundles -> 13
    1. Twigs square … Phygelius
    2. Twigs round -> 15
  7. Not net veined, weak 2 pinnate veins. Leaves with yellow blotches above, petiole green … Aucuba (japonica)
  8. Net veined and strongly 3-pli-veined. No yellow blotches, petiole reddish … Viburnum
    1. Young twigs brown, each leaf tooth with a fragile claw like gland … Euonymus
    2. Young twigs green, leaf teeth without glands -> 17
  9. Leaves cuneate at base, buds with scales … Rhamnus
  10. Leaves rounded at base, buds naked … Phillyrea

Key 4.3: Deciduous plants

  1. Bundle trace 1 -> Key 4.3.1
  2. Bundle traces >1 -> 5
    1. Bundle traces 3 -> Key 4.3.2
    2. Bundle traces >3 -> Key 4.3.3

Key 4.3.1: Bundle trace 1

  1. Twigs green … Euonymus
  2. Twigs not green when mature -> 3
    1. Trees -> 5
    2. Shrubs -> 9
  3. Terminal buds always present, buds felted brown, grey or black … Fraxinus
  4. Terminal buds absent -> 11
    1. Pith chambered, twigs densly hairy … Paulownia (tomentosa)
    2. Pith solid, twigs red brown… Metasequoia (glyptostryboides)
  5. Buds sometimes whorled in 3’s… Fuschia
  6. Buds strictly opposite -> 11
    1. Pith present, solid, whitish -> 13
    2. Twigs hollow or chambered, brownish -> 15
  7. Buds mostly in pairs at twig apices, glandular ciliate with 6-7 scales … Syringa
  8. Buds singular at twig apices, ciliate with ± 4 pairs of opposite scales … Ligustrum
    1. Buds >11 pairs of scales, twigs chambered to hollow between nodes … Forsythia
    2. Buds <4 pairs of scales, or absent, twigs hollow but never chambered -> 17
  9. Leaf scars mostly torn, partly connected by transverse ridges, buds with keeled scales. Fruits white or red berries … Symphoricarpos
  10. Leaf scars never torn, triangularly lens shaped, not partly connected by ridges, buds without scales … Hypericum

Key 4.3.2: Bundle traces 3

  1. Transverse ridge not present between buds or leaf scars -> 3
  2. Transverse ridge present between buds or leaf scars, or leaf scars abut -> 5
    1. Shoots thorny, buds with 8-10 dark brown scales, terminal buds present… Rhamnus (cathartica)
    2. Shoots not thorny, buds red with one scale visible, terminal buds absent… Cercidiphyllum (japonicum)
  3. Buds hidden behind leaf scar (which is like a thin membrane)… Philadelphus
  4. Buds exposed and obvious -> 7
    1. Buds naked -> 9
    2. Buds with scales -> 13
  5. Only terminal bud naked, twigs stout… Hydrangea
  6. All buds naked -> 11
    1. Twigs but especially buds with stellate hairs… Viburnum
    2. Twigs and buds without stellate hairs but with medifixed hairs… Cornus
  7. Stipule scars large and obvious… Staphylea
  8. Stipule scars absent or not obvious -> 15
    1. Buds with a pair of scales fused, enveloping bud, buds globose, red. Or flowering in winter, fragrant… Viburnum
    2. Bud scales >2, not fused, not flowering in winter -> 17
  9. Pith hollow between nodes -> 19
  10. Pith solid, or spongey -> 23
    1. Buds solitary, twigs green … Leycesteria
    2. Buds often superposed or collateral, twigs never green -> 21
  11. Buds >3mm long, with buds often superposed… Lonicera
  12. Buds <3mm long, buds often collateral… Symphoricarpos
    1. Twigs with 2-4 ridges decurrent from nodes. 2-valved capsules persistent … Weigela
    2. Twigs without ridges decurrent from nodes, fruits rarely persistent -> 25
  13. Bundle traces forming a line, fruits persistent, bristly with 5 lobed calyx…Kolkwitzia
  14. Bundle traces distinct, fruits not persistent, or if they are, not as above -> 27
    1. Pith spongey, twigs stout… Sambucus
    2. Pith solid, twigs more slender… Acer

Key 4.3.3: Bundle traces >3

  1. Leaf bases obscuring leaf scars -> 3
  2. Leaf scars clearly present -> 5
    1. Twigs green, hollow… Leycesteria (formosana)
    2. Twigs red or purplish, solid… Acer (section Palmata)
  3. At least some nodes in whorls of 3 -> 7
  4. Nodes strictly opposite -> 11
    1. Bundle traces >8 in an ellipse or horseshoe shape, terminal bud absent -> 9
    2. Bundle traces < 7 (usually 3-5), terminal bud present… Hydrangea
  5. Each node with one small leaf scar, and two large… Catalpa (bignonioides)
  6. Leaf scars all the same size… Clerodendron (trichotomum)
    1. Large stipule scars between leaf scars… Staphylea (pinnata)
    2. If stipule scars present, inconspicuous -> 13
  7. Trees -> 15
  8. Shrubs -> 21
    1. Terminal bud present, pith solid -> 17
    2. Terminal bud absent, pith chambered… Paulownia (tomentosa)
  9. Bundle traces many in a closed circle, buds furry… Fraxinus
  10. Bundle traces <9, distinct, buds not furry but may be hairy -> 19
    1. Terminal buds >15mm, leaf scars large, shield shaped… Aesculus
    2. Terminal buds < 15mm, leaf scars smaller, mostly crescent shaped… Acer
  11. Terminal bud very large (15-20mm), naked… Hydrangea
  12. Terminal bud smaller <15mm, often absent from shoots -> 23
    1. Pith wide, spongey, twigs stout … Sambucus
    2. Pith narrower, hard, more slender … Acer
