Winter Botany in the British Isles

Key 3: Leaves or leaf scars whorled

  1. Leaves evergreen -> 3
  2. Leaves deciduous, leaf scars apparent -> 15
    1. Leaves pseudowhorled -> 5
    2. Leaves in true whorls -> 7
  3. Matted evergreen shrub < 0.4m … Empetrum
  4. Tall shrub to small tree >2m … Rhododendron
    1. Leaves 3 whorled -> 9
    2. Leaves >4 whorled -> 11
  5. Young twigs glandular hairy … Erica
  6. Young twigs not glandular hairy -> 11
    1. Leaves > 10mm wide … Kalmia
    2. Leaves < 1mm wide … Erica
  7. Leaves tough >6mm wide, strongly retrorsely scabrid … Rubia
  8. Lvs ± 1mm wide … Erica
    1. Leaf scars minute, many, alternate, raised on spurs on second year shoots, reproductive organs cones … Larix
    2. Leaf scars larger, 3 per node, not raised -> 17
  9. Twigs stout, leaf scars large with ~ 12 bundle traces in an ellipse. Buds usually arranged in two large and one small … Catalpa (bignonioides)
  10. Twigs slender, leaf scars smaller with 3 or fewer bundle traces -> 19
    1. Bundle trace 1, twigs mostly dead… Fuschia
    2. Bundle traces 3 -> 21
  11. Twigs with stellate scales … Deutzia (better characters?)
  12. Twigs hairless or with simple hairs … Hydrangea
